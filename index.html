Intro

Where creativity meets technology.

My dream had always been to be some form of a graphic designer/copywriter, using my creative skillset to draw and write in cute coffee shops (or from the couch) for a living. With that goal in mind, I gravitated towards custom design projects and freelance work, eventually starting my own business. While I enjoyed doing a wide range of different custom works, I felt like there was a lack of consistency from project to project and I wasn't being challenged through my work.

Growing up I'd always been interested in video games. Wondering how they worked, what made them tick, how the art/assets were made. But most importantly, how I could get into making them. Those questions led to the discovery that to make any form of a game I needed to learn to code. And here we are! I'm working toward finding a balance between illustrative design and programming.

Game Development merges my graphic design background with my software engineering skills. Incorporating creativity and problem solving, promoting open-minded thinking and adaptability. I love being able to create my own games, as well as using my own art, learning as I go and improving anywhere I can.

My strengths include background concept design, asset creation, creative and critical thinking, time-management, teamwork and collaboration. I absolutely love C++, C# and using Git/Github. I have a bit of HTML and CSS under my belt as well.

Currently, I am revamping my art portfolio and taking Unity's Junior Programmer Certification course. I'm enthusiastically building my repertoire and excited about the opportunities ahead.

Coding

For the longest time coding was something I thought I could never do. It seemed too complicated for a mere illustrator to be able to pick up and learn on the fly. So I put it off. For YEARS. Finally, toward the end of 2021 I decided to figure out just how much work it would be to learn one language. I enrolled in my first Codecademy course to Learn C++ and I haven't looked back!

I've been adding to my languages repertoire and building my projects portfolio as I go. Something that I really enjoy about learning to code, is that there's no shortage of concepts to grasp and understand.

My two strongest languages by far are C++ and C#. I love the vast functionality of both. Currently I'm working mostly with C# in the Unity engine, learning the ins and outs of game development.

Other languages I know are HTML and CSS. I also am familiar with using Git/Github for source control. If you're interested in looking through my projects you can find my Github linked below.
